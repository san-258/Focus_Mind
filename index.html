Morning breath anchor
Box breathing · 3 min
7–8 AM

Sets a calm, intentional baseline before cognitive work. Box breathing activates the parasympathetic system, reducing cortisol and priming prefrontal focus.

  • 1 Sit upright before opening any screen
  • 2 Inhale 4 counts → hold 4 → exhale 4 → hold 4
  • 3 Repeat for 3 minutes (~8 cycles)
  • 4 Set one clear intention for the day

Single-task focus session
Mindful transition · 5 min
Deep work

Before each deep work block, a mindful transition anchors your attention to one task only. Most "distraction" is unresolved residue from the previous task.

  • 1 Close all tabs except the one you need
  • 2 Name the task — write it down or say it aloud
  • 3 Take 5 slow breaths; notice any urge to switch
  • 4 Work 25 min; gently return when attention wanders

Midday reset
Body scan + breath · 5 min
Lunch

Clears accumulated mental residue from the morning. Holding tension in your jaw, shoulders, or lower back is cognitive overhead — releasing it reclaims mental bandwidth.

  • 1 Stand or lie down; close your eyes
  • 2 Scan: head → jaw → shoulders → hands → lower back
  • 3 Release held tension with each exhale
  • 4 Finish with 5 deep belly breaths

Evening wind-down
Journaling reflection · 5 min
End of day

Closes open cognitive loops that keep your mind "on" after work. Trains pattern recognition about what conditions produce your best focus.

  • 1 Write 3 things you completed today
  • 2 Note one moment your focus felt sharp — what caused it?
  • 3 Write tomorrow's single most important task
  • 4 Close laptop; do a 1-min breath cool-down
